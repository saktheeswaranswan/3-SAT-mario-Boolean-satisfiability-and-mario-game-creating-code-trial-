# Comprehensive Architectural and Assembly Specifications: Project Surreal Miniature Diorama
## Document Control and Preamble
This document constitutes the exhaustive, definitive architectural blueprint, material science specification, and assembly manual for the physical construction of the hyper-realistic surreal miniature diorama (Project Reference: Image_2 / 60133 / 60130). This is a strict construction control document. All spatial coordinates (mapped on a three-dimensional Cartesian plane where the bottom-left corner of the external frame's front face is designated as the origin point X=0, Y=0, Z=0), material formulations, entity relationships, and optical parameters detailed herein are absolute. Deviations from these specifications constitute a critical structural, aesthetic, or conceptual failure.
## Phase 1: Definitive Structural Boundaries and Entryway Identification
### Section 1.1: The Macro-Frame (Terracotta Masonry)
The outermost perimeter serves as the foundational load-bearing macro-frame. It establishes an unbreakable 1:1 square boundary, measuring precisely 24.000 inches by 24.000 inches externally, with an internal negative space (the diorama theater) of 20.000 inches by 20.000 inches.
 * **Dimensional Tolerances:** The overall frame depth (Z-axis) is strictly 4.000 inches. All dimensional tolerances are \pm 0.031 inches to maintain orthographic perfection under macro photography.
 * **Brick Fabrication and Formulation:** The perimeter is constructed from custom-cast, high-density terracotta clay. The raw clay body must be a grog-heavy (20% grog by volume for dimensional stability) red earthenware, fired in a highly controlled oxidation atmosphere to a peak temperature of Cone 04 (1945°F / 1063°C). Each individual brick must measure exactly 4.00 inches (length) by 2.00 inches (depth/Z-axis) by 1.00 inch (height).
 * **Albedo and Weathering Chemistry:** The base color must not be uniform. It requires a primary albedo of oxidized crimson (strictly matched to Pantone 18-1662 TCX) intermixed with burnt sienna. Post-firing, each brick must undergo a multi-layered mechanical and chemical weathering process:
   1. **Tumbling:** Bricks must be mechanically tumbled with ceramic media for 45 minutes to achieve authentic edge-wear and tiny corner chips (micro-spalling).
   2. **Acid Wash:** A 5% muriatic acid solution wash must be applied to create unique deep pores, microscopic pits, and micro-fissures, neutralizing immediately with a sodium bicarbonate bath.
   3. **Pigment Wash:** Scattered highlights of desaturated pale orange and ochre must be applied using highly diluted, fast-drying acrylic washes, stippled to simulate centuries of physical weight, atmospheric dust, and localized mineral efflorescence.
 * **Mortar Application and Curing:** The binding agent is a custom dry-mix mortar.
   * **Formulation:** 1 part white Portland cement, 0.5 parts hydrated lime, and 3 parts coarse, unwashed silica sand (to maintain a porous, gritty texture). Pigment with 2% black iron oxide to achieve a sandy, off-white, light-grey tone.
   * **Application:** Apply in a classic running bond pattern. The vertical columns on the left and right must be exactly two brick-lengths wide (8.00 inches total external width per column).
   * **Tooling:** Mortar lines must be deeply recessed (raked joints). Exactly 45 minutes after laying, joints must be mechanically raked to a depth of 0.25 inches using a standard masonry jointer, followed by aggressive brushing with a stiff bristle brush to induce a crumbling, aged texture.
### Section 1.2: The Primary Informational Plaque (Entryway ID)
The entryway identification plaque serves as the semantic anchor for the diorama, requiring precision machining and flawless installation.
 * **Spatial Coordinates:** Anchored in the top-left corner of the diorama's internal theater. The top-left corner of the plaque itself is positioned at X = 1.00 inch, Y = 23.50 inches, Z = 4.00 inches (flush with the front face of the bricks). It must physically overlap the top and left brick borders and extend precisely 0.50 inches downward into the open sky backdrop space.
 * **Material Metallurgy:** Machined from a solid billet of 304 stainless steel or aerospace-grade anisotropic brushed aluminum (6061-T6 alloy). Dimensions are precisely 3.000 inches wide by 1.000 inch high, with a thickness of 1.00 mm.
 * **Surface Finish:** The surface must exhibit a high-grain anisotropic brushed finish. The brushing must be perfectly horizontal (parallel to the X-axis) using a 400-grit abrasive belt, ensuring a subtle, consistent grain line with absolute zero cross-hatching. Edges must be mechanically finished via CNC milling to achieve flawless 0.50 mm radii.
 * **Typography and Engraving:**
   * **Text:** "Variable gadget: Left (False)".
   * **Font:** A sterile, highly legible modern sans-serif typeface (Helvetica Neue LT Pro or Roboto, Regular weight).
   * **Process:** Precision fiber-laser engraving. The laser must ablate the metal to a strictly controlled depth of 1.50 mm.
   * **Backfilling:** The recessed text channels must be manually backfilled using a two-part, ultra-matte, light-absorbing black epoxy resin. The epoxy must be cured under vacuum to eliminate micro-bubbles, then leveled perfectly flush with the brushed metal surface.
 * **Mechanical Attachment:** The plaque is affixed using exactly four #1-72 stainless steel Phillips-head machine screws.
   * **Placement:** Screws are located precisely 0.125 inches from each respective corner.
   * **Execution:** Holes must be precision-drilled and countersunk into the plaque. The screws must be driven perfectly flush with the plaque surface. The underlying brick must be hammer-drilled and fitted with micro-anchors. Localized, soft-edged drop shadows must be verified where the plaque physically overhangs the brick and the spatial void.
## Phase 2: Internal Confectionery and Masonry Matrix
This phase dictates the core architecture of the surreal landscape, demanding rigorous adherence to material science regarding lipid crystallization and structural engineering on a miniature scale.
### Section 2.1: Material Physics for Confectionery Structures
The confectionery elements are not mere representations; they are physical materials requiring exact thermal profiling to achieve specific optical properties.
 * **Edible Chocolate Formulation (Standard Pips and Paths):**
   * **Composition:** A precise 38.5% cocoa solids milk chocolate formulation, characterized by a high milk fat content to ensure structural malleability before curing.
   * **Tempering Physics:** The chocolate must undergo strict beta-V crystal tempering. Melt to 45°C (113°F), rapidly cool to 27°C (80.6°F) under constant shear, and gently re-warm to 30°C (86°F). This specific Time-Temperature curve guarantees a slightly glossy, waxy surface finish with a target gloss meter reading of 45-50 GU at a 60-degree angle.
   * **Optical Profile (SSS):** The material must prioritize deep Subsurface Scattering. The internal radiant exitance relies on a dipole diffusion profile where the scattering coefficient (\sigma_s) heavily outweighs the absorption coefficient (\sigma_a) in the red and green wavelengths, yielding a warm, internal glow under studio lighting.
   * **Molding:** Precision-milled food-grade silicone molds must be used to cast breakable, confectionery-bar-style pips. Each pip block (measuring 1.00 x 1.00 x 1.00 inch) must feature a sharply recessed, 45-degree beveled center, forming a secondary inner square measuring 0.60 x 0.60 inches.
 * **Dark Chocolate Formulation (Checkerboard Monolith):**
   * **Composition:** A rigid, structurally sound 70% minimum cocoa content dark chocolate.
   * **Tempering and Optics:** Tempered at a higher working temperature of 31.5°C (88.7°F). This formulation utilizes a different SSS dipole diffusion profile, possessing a much higher absorption coefficient across all visible wavelengths, resulting in a deeper, richer black-brown color and a significantly lower gloss level (target 15-20 GU at 60 degrees), presenting as a nearly matte, monolithic surface.
### Section 2.2: Internal Red Masonry (Paths and Supports)
The internal masonry utilizes the exact same custom-cast terracotta clay and porous mortar defined in Section 1.1, but requires precision cutting via wet-saw into custom, miniaturized segments (e.g., half-bricks measuring 2.00 x 2.00 x 1.00 inches) to integrate with the chocolate matrix.
### Section 2.3: Step-by-Step Matrix Assembly and Spatial Coordinates
The internal theater (bounding box: X=2.00 to 22.00, Y=2.00 to 22.00) is populated as follows:
 * **Step 1: The Primary Vertical Left Pillar and Inner Channel.**
   * Construct an upside-down U-shaped structural column using standard red bricks.
   * **Origin:** The base of the pillar begins at X=2.00, Y=2.00, rising vertically flush against the inner left frame wall.
   * **Structure:** It rises vertically to Y=14.00 inches. It extends horizontally inward to X=6.00, then descends vertically back to Y=4.00, leaving a 2.00-inch wide inner channel (from X=4.00 to X=6.00) between the primary wall and the inner column.
   * **Platform:** The top surface (Y=14.00, from X=2.00 to X=8.00) forms a flat, contiguous brick ledge.
 * **Step 2: The Upper Canopy Maze Paths.**
   * This complex, interwoven system acts as a suspended structural lattice.
   * **Left Connection:** A horizontal milk chocolate path begins at the left frame wall (X=2.00, Y=18.00) and extends to X=6.00.
   * **Brick Integration:** From X=6.00, Y=18.00, a terracotta brick path zig-zags upwards: extending right to X=8.00, up to Y=20.00, right to X=10.00, down to Y=17.00.
   * **Right Connection:** The canopy seamlessly anchors into the right monolithic structure and the right outer frame wall at Y=18.00. The maze contains intentional dead ends (e.g., a vertical drop terminating at X=9.00, Y=16.00).
 * **Step 3: The Dark and Milk Chocolate Checkerboard Monolith.**
   * This massive retaining wall anchors the right side of the diorama.
   * **Dimensions:** It rises contiguously from the bottom brick floor (Y=2.00) up to a height of Y=14.00, terminating precisely level with the left brick platform. It spans horizontally from X=12.00 to X=22.00 (flush against the right inner frame wall).
   * **Pattern:** Constructed as a precise, alternating grid. Using 2.00 x 2.00 inch blocks, it forms a 5-column by 6-row matrix. The pattern must strictly alternate dark (70%) and milk (38.5%) formulations. The coordinate block at (X=12.00, Y=2.00) must be dark chocolate.
   * **Cap Layer:** The absolute top layer of this monolith (Y=14.00 to Y=15.00) must be constructed entirely of detailed milk chocolate blocks to visually bridge the monolith with the interwoven canopy maze above.
 * **Step 4: Custom Milk Chocolate Zig-Zag Paths (Top Canopy).**
   * Located at the very top of the structural hierarchy.
   * **Coordinates:** A path originates from the center-right of the upper maze (X=14.00, Y=20.00), extends right to X=18.00, drops down one block length to Y=18.00, and forms a precise right-angle terminal bracket designed specifically to encapsulate the Question Block.
 * **Step 5: The Wide Vertical Central Void Gap.**
   * **Boundaries:** This negative space is defined by X=8.00 on the left (the edge of the left brick pillar) and X=12.00 on the right (the edge of the checkerboard monolith).
   * **Extent:** It extends completely unobstructed vertically from the Y=14.00 ledge down to the bottom floor bricks at Y=2.00, forming a 4.00-inch wide chasm.
 * **Step 6: The Staggered Floating Steps.**
   * Precisely installed within the central chasm (X=8.00 to 12.00).
   * **Specification:** These are strictly isolated, floating platforms. Each step consists of exactly one customized milk chocolate pip segment, measuring 1.50 inches wide by 0.50 inches high by 1.00 inch deep.
   * **Positioning:** They are arranged in a descending, alternating zig-zag pattern exactly down the centerline of the void. They do not physically connect to the walls; their suspension must be achieved invisibly from the rear (Z-axis).
   * **Coordinates:**
     * Step 1 (Highest): X=10.00, Y=12.00
     * Step 2: X=9.00, Y=10.00
     * Step 3: X=10.50, Y=8.00
     * Step 4: X=9.50, Y=6.00
     * Step 5 (Lowest): X=10.00, Y=4.00
## Phase 3: Diorama Inhabitants, Devices, and Artefacts
This phase outlines the specific entities occupying the architecture. Exacting standards for scale, anatomical posing, texture replication, and paint application must be observed.
### Entity A: The Human Scale Component
The primary narrative anchor, providing a sense of scale and surreal juxtaposition.
 * **Coordinates:** Positioned at (X_man=5.50 inches, Y_man=14.00 inches, Z_man=3.00 inches). He stands securely on the upper left brick platform ledge, slightly set back from the front edge.
 * **Manufacturing and Scale:** A precision resin-cast humanoid figurine. The scale is strictly 1:16, resulting in an exact physical height of 4.375 inches. The casting resin must be a high-shore-hardness polyurethane to allow for microscopic detail retention.
 * **Painting Methodology:** Requires a multi-step hand-painting process under magnification.
   * **Primer:** Ultra-fine light grey lacquer primer.
   * **Skin Tones:** Layered acrylic glazes (minimum 5 layers) to achieve realistic subsurface dermal tones, avoiding a flat, plastic appearance.
 * **Attire Specifications:**
   * **Shirt:** A pale coral/peach button-down collared shirt. The color must match Pantone 15-1322 TCX. The sculpture *must* feature a highly detailed, realistic cotton fabric weave texture (specifically mimicking a standard Oxford cloth weave, characterized by a basket-weave structure) sculpted directly into the resin. The sleeves must be casually rolled precisely to the mid-forearm, exhibiting realistic fabric bunching and tension folds.
   * **Trousers:** Dark grey denim jeans. Must feature a sculpted faded denim twill texture. Critical detail: the jeans must exhibit realistic "stacking" (fabric pooling and folding) around the ankles where they meet the footwear.
   * **Footwear:** Classic brown leather derby shoes, painted with a semi-gloss varnish to simulate polished leather.
 * **Pose and Demeanor:** The pose must be completely neutral, casual, and static. Weight must be evenly balanced on both legs (no contrapposto). Arms hang relaxed at his sides, fingers naturally curled. The head is turned slightly to face camera-left, gazing outward from the box with an impassive, observant expression.
### Entity B: The Active CRT Device
A technologically functional, miniature electronic prop that introduces active light into the static scene.
 * **Coordinates:** Positioned at (X_screen=6.50 inches, Y_screen=14.00 inches, Z_screen=3.25 inches), resting on the left brick ledge immediately next to and slightly forward of the human's feet.
 * **Casing Assembly:** A distinct, perfectly cubic casing measuring 1.25 x 1.25 x 1.25 inches.
   * **Material:** Cobalt blue (Pantone 2748 C) matte-finish painted brass or CNC-milled PVC-free high-density polyethylene (HDPE).
   * **Detailing:** Edges must be micro-milled with a 0.5mm chamfer. The casing must appear industrially manufactured.
 * **Screen Module:**
   * The front face features a custom-milled recessed aperture (0.80 x 0.80 inches), set back 0.125 inches from the front face.
   * **Technology:** Integrate a functional, miniature backlit LED array or a micro-OLED screen. If using an LED array, it must sit behind a dark, tinted acrylic diffuser panel to mimic the black state of a CRT tube.
 * **Typography and Emission:**
   * **Display:** The screen must actively display the text "I LIVE".
   * **Font:** A strict, blocky, 8-bit retro-digital all-caps font (e.g., VT323 or similar pixel font).
   * **Illumination:** The text must emit a phosphorescent neon green light (Peak wavelength ~525 nm). The luminance must be calibrated to cast a localized, measurable ambient green light wash onto the adjacent brickwork and the right side of the human's trousers. Internal wiring (30 AWG enameled copper wire) must be routed invisibly through the bottom of the casing, through a micro-drill hole in the brick, and out the back of the diorama to an external 3V DC power source.

Eyes are circular, bright white, with small, intensely staring black pupils positioned to gaze aggressively forward and slightly down. The mouth requires two sharp, conical fangs painted ivory white, protruding upward over the lower jawline.
 * **Base Assembly (Mechanical/Botanical):**

   * **Form:** The head must rest upon a precision-engineered yellow, multi-petaled propeller or flower-like botanical-mechanical hybrid base unit. Base diameter is 1.25 inches.

### Entity E: The Gold Hoard and Specular Surface
A study in extreme specular reflection and chaotic arrangement against a textured, matte background.
 * **Coordinates:** Positioned at (X_gold=10.00 inches, Y_gold=2.00 inches, Z_gold=3.00 inches), located at the exact bottom center of the wide central chasm, resting directly on the bottom horizontal terracotta brick border.
 * **Gold Coins (The Currency):**
   * **Quantity:** Exactly 25 individual coins must be installed.
   * **Manufacturing:** Precision CNC-milled or heavily stamped from a solid brass core. Each coin must measure exactly 0.375 inches in diameter and 0.0625 inches in thickness. Edge reeding (the ridges on the side) must be mathematically precise.

They must act as perfect optical reflectors in the lighting setup.
   * **Arrangement:** The 25 coins must not be stacked neatly. They must be permanently affixed in a specific, haphazard, unorganized, and chaotic scattered pile.

Prior to affixing the coins, apply loose mortar particles, micro-cracks in the terracotta, and a slight accumulation of simulated dust to heavily contrast the pristine gold against the ancient floor.
## Phase 4: Environmental and Photometry Specifications
This phase dictates the physical environment surrounding the architecture and the strict optical parameters required to capture it correctly.
### Section 4.1: Physical Backdrop Assembly
The illusion of a sky is achieved through physical media, expressly forbidding digital compositing or infinite-depth rendering techniques.
 * **Materials:** The backdrop must be constructed from a single, seamless, rigid sheet of 1/4-inch high-density styrene card (dimensions: 24.00 x 24.00 inches).
 * **Surface Finish and Gradient:**
   * The styrene must be primed with a matte gesso.
   * The sky requires a flawless, smooth color gradient applied via HVLP (High Volume Low Pressure) airbrush.
   * The gradient must transition seamlessly from a deeper cerulean blue (Pantone 293 U) at the absolute top edge, fading downward in a linear progression to a softer, paler, highly atmospheric light blue (Pantone 290 U) at the bottom edge.
 * **Cloud Application:** Exactly 5 stylized, fluffy, opaque white cumulus clouds must be hand-painted onto the gradient. The paint must be a heavy-body titanium white acrylic, applied to leave a very subtle, raised brushstroke texture. The clouds must be distributed evenly across the mid-to-upper Y-axis of the backdrop.
 * **Spatial Positioning:** The finished backdrop card must be mounted perfectly parallel to the main structural matrix, set back precisely 2.00 inches (Z-axis offset) behind the rearmost edge of the internal brick and chocolate structures. This physical separation is mandatory to allow for the casting of actual shadows. The visual appearance of infinite atmospheric depth is strictly prohibited.
### Section 4.2: Illumination (Primary Photometry)
The lighting setup requires a definitive, multi-point studio configuration utilizing continuous, high-CRI (Color Rendering Index > 98) LED light sources.
 * **Key Light:**
   * **Source:** A large, 12.00 x 12.00 inch LED panel equipped with a double-baffled softbox and a 40-degree egg-crate grid to control spill.
   * **Position:** Placed overhead, originating from the top-left quadrant relative to the diorama. It must be angled precisely at 45 degrees downward toward the center point of the diorama, positioned exactly 12.00 inches away from the front plane (Z=0).
   * **Color Temperature:** Strictly daylight balanced at 5500 Kelvin.
   * **Effect:** This primary light must be calibrated to produce specific, gentle, highly rounded specular highlights on the upper bevels of the individual milk chocolate pips, revealing their waxy, tempered surface.
 * **Fill Light:**
   * **Source:** A smaller LED panel with a heavy full-stop silk diffusion gel.
   * **Position:** Placed front-right, slightly below the horizontal center axis.
   * **Intensity:** Must be metered to precisely 2.5 stops below the Key Light output.
   * **Purpose:** To elevate the black point and prevent entirely crushed shadows in the deep recesses. It is critical that this fill light is strong enough to ensure the dark chocolate (70%) checkerboard squares on the right monolith retain visible color information, specular detail, and texture, rather than falling into pure black.
 * **Rim/Kicker Light:**
   * **Source:** A hard, focused optical snoot attached to a small LED point source (5500K).
   * **Position:** Placed at the rear-left, firing forward and across the scene.
   * **Effect:** Must create a razor-thin, bright specular rim light catching the inner right-facing edges of the left brick frame and pillar. Crucially, it must physically separate the dark silhouette of the human figure and the blue CRT casing from the painted background.
   * **Ambient Wash Validation:** Independently verify that the phosphorescent green emission from the CRT ("I LIVE") casts a localized, measurable green color spill onto the adjacent brick floor and the side of the human's left shoe and pant leg.
 * **Shadow Casting (Critical Requirement):**
   * The lighting ratios must produce distinct, softly defined drop shadows cast backward onto the physical styrene backdrop card from *all* foreground elements.
   * The most critical area for shadow validation is the central void gap; the staggered, floating chocolate steps must cast individual, clearly defined diagonal shadows onto the blue sky backdrop, falling toward the lower right.
   * Complex contact shadows (Ambient Occlusion) must be rich and dense where physical objects meet: between the aluminum base of the Goomba and the chocolate platform, between the human's leather shoes and the porous brick, and deep within the chaotic pile of gold coins on the ledge.
### Section 4.3: Photography and Optical Configuration
The final capture requires absolute orthographic precision and specific depth-of-field manipulation.
 * **Hardware:** A full-frame, high-resolution digital camera body (minimum 45 megapixels, e.g., Canon EOS R5 or Phase One equivalent).
 * **Lens:** A 100mm Macro lens. Alternatively, a 90mm or 135mm Tilt-Shift lens may be employed to ensure zero perspective distortion.
 * **Positioning:** The camera sensor plane must be aligned perfectly parallel to the front face of the diorama. The lens axis must be perpendicular (a perfect 90-degree angle) to the exact physical center of the 20x20 inner void (X=12.00, Y=12.00). This guarantees a completely flat, orthographic view where the inner side walls are minimally visible.
 * **Lens Parameters and Depth of Field:**
   * **Aperture:** Locked strictly at f/8.0. This specific aperture is chosen to create a meticulously calculated, shallow depth of field.
   * **Focal Plane:** The razor-sharp point of critical focus *must* be manually locked onto the leading edge of the human figure's face/shirt, the front face of the blue CRT box, and the immediately adjacent front edges of the left brick pillar.
   * **Bokeh/Defocus:** All elements located spatially behind this critical plane (most notably, the painted clouds on the styrene backdrop) and in front of this plane (the outermost edges of the 4-inch deep external frame bricks) must exhibit a subtle, mathematically precise, creamy optical blur (bokeh). The backdrop must not be in sharp focus, further enhancing the miniature tilt-shift illusion.
## Phase 5: Quality Control, Exclusions, and Non-Conformity Protocols
The following parameters are critical pass/fail conditions for the physical build and final capture. Any deviation, no matter how minor, constitutes a critical Quality Control (QC) failure, requiring a complete rebuild or reshoot of the affected section. Inspectors must use appropriate metrology equipment (calipers, spectrophotometers, gloss meters) to verify these conditions.
 1. **QC Failure Condition 1: Infinite Depth Backdrop.** The backdrop must be verified as a tangible, rigid physical styrene card positioned 2 inches behind the matrix. The presence of physical drop shadows cast upon it by the foreground architecture is the primary validation mechanism. Any post-production manipulation or physical setup resulting in the appearance of a boundless, infinite outdoor horizon, an atmospheric perspective fade to white, or a glowing sun is a critical failure.
 2. **QC Failure Condition 2: Melted Chocolate Architecture.** The confectionery elements (both 38.5% and 70% formulations) must maintain strict, hard-edged architectural integrity. Any visual evidence of thermal degradation, including melting, a liquid or viscous appearance, softened bevels, drooping spans, or dripping/pooling of chocolate onto the masonry below, is strictly non-conformist. The ambient temperature of the studio must be maintained below 18°C (64°F) during the shoot to prevent this failure.
 3. **QC Failure Condition 3: Standard Goomba Anatomy.** The presence of standard, anatomical, brown, rounded, or shoe-like feet on the Goomba entity (Entity C) is prohibited. The base must be exclusively the precision-machined, multi-petaled yellow botanical/mechanical propeller unit, finished in matte anodized aluminum. Any deviation from this specific hybrid design is a failure.
 4. **QC Failure Condition 4: Typographical Errors.** Exacting proofreading is required for all text.
   * The entry plaque must read exactly: "Variable gadget: Left (False)". Verify capitalization of 'V' and 'L', the colon, the space after the colon, and the parentheses.
   * The CRT screen must read exactly: "I LIVE". Verify all-caps, the space between words, and the absence of punctuation. Any deviation in spelling, casing, or punctuation is a failure.
 5. **QC Failure Condition 5: Wardrobe Non-Conformity.** The human entity's attire will be verified via colorimeter and macro-lens inspection. The shirt must strictly be pale coral/peach (matching Pantone 15-1322 TCX) and must physically exhibit the specified sculpted cotton/Oxford weave texture. The jeans must be dark grey with a sculpted faded denim twill texture and visible ankle stacking. Smooth clothing or incorrect colorways (e.g., blue jeans, white shirt) will fail inspection.
 6. **QC Failure Condition 6: Unapproved Characters.** The diorama is a closed ecosystem. The presence of any recognized intellectual property protagonists or antagonists other than the specifically modified hybrid Goomba is a failure. Verify the absolute absence of Mario, Luigi, Bowser, Peach, Toads, or any other unauthorized figures.
 7. **QC Failure Condition 7: Alternate Masonry Materials.** The structural masonry (macro-frame and internal paths) must be verified as authentic, fired terracotta clay. The use of substitute materials such as cast concrete, smooth river stone, standard grey cinderblock, carved foam painted to look like brick, or 3D printed plastics is a critical material non-conformity.
 8. **QC Failure Condition 8: Dull Currency.** The gold coins (Entity E) must exhibit a high-specular, flawless mirror reflection characteristic of polished 24-karat gold plating. The presence of dull, matte, oxidized, antiqued, or bronze-toned coins that fail to reflect the studio lighting is non-conformist.
 9. **QC Failure Condition 9: Solid Middle Wall or Ladder.** The central 4.00-inch wide void gap must remain open space (open sky to the backdrop), populated *exclusively* by the alternating, staggered, floating chocolate steps detailed in Phase 2, Step 6. The presence of a solid chocolate wall filling this gap, vertical brick columns within the gap, or standard wooden/rope ladders connecting the left and right sides is a structural failure.
 10. **QC Failure Condition 10: Unapproved Signatures and Digital Artifacts.** All physical surfaces and the final photographic capture must be absolutely pristine. Verify the complete absence of physical artist signatures painted onto the frame, embedded watermarks, AI rendering artifacts (such as nonsensical geometric merging, floating disjointed pixels, or blurred boundaries between distinct materials), or digital text overlays not explicitly specified in this document. The final image must represent a pure, unadulterated physical capture of the specified diorama.
